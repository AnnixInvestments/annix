# API 650 Welded Tanks for Oil Storage Reference

> Source: API 650 12th Edition (2013)
> This document contains key reference data extracted for use by the Annix quoting application and Nix document processor.

## 1. Scope

API 650 covers the design, fabrication, erection, and testing of vertical, cylindrical, aboveground, closed- and open-top, welded storage tanks in various sizes and capacities for internal pressures approximating atmospheric pressure.

## 3. Tank Nozzle Requirements

### 3.1 Nozzle Pipe Materials (Section 4.5)

For shell nozzles and manhole necks:
- Seamless pipe per ASTM A53, A106, A333, A524
- Seamless forgings
- Welded pipe per ASTM A671 (with additional requirements)

**Material Group Requirements:**
- Group IV, IVA, V, or VI: Seamless pipe or rolled/forged shapes required
- Impact testing required per Table 4.4 and 4.5

### 3.2 Pipe Specifications for Nozzles (Section 4.5.1.1)

Acceptable specifications for shell nozzles:
- ASTM A53 Grade B (seamless or ERW)
- ASTM A106 Grade B
- ASTM A333 Grade 6 (low temperature)
- ASTM A524 Grade I or II
- ASTM A671 (with restrictions)

### 3.3 Nozzle Neck Thickness

Per Section 4.5:
- Shell nozzle necks: minimum of Schedule 40 or 6mm (1/4") whichever is greater
- For nominal pipe sizes > NPS 24: See Table 5.7a/5.7b

## 4. Flange Types for Tank Nozzles

### 4.1 Acceptable Flange Types

| Flange Type | Application | Weld Size Requirement |
|-------------|-------------|----------------------|
| Slip-on Flange | General service | tn or Tf (lesser) |
| Ring-type Flange | General service | tn or Tf (lesser) |
| Welding-neck Flange | High integrity joints | tn |
| Long Welding-neck Flange | Extended nozzles | tn or ts (lesser) |
| Lap-type Joint Flange | Easy alignment | tn or Tf (lesser) |

Where:
- ts = shell thickness
- tn = nozzle neck thickness
- Tf = flange thickness

### 4.2 Flange Weld Size Requirements

**Slip-on Flanges:**
- Inside weld: Full penetration or fillet weld throat = tn
- Outside weld: Fillet weld throat = tn

**Welding-neck Flanges:**
- Neck-to-pipe weld: Full penetration groove weld
- Does not exceed 19mm (3/4 in.) without PWHT

## 5. Nozzle Fabrication Tolerances (Section 7.5)

### 5.1 Shell Nozzle Tolerances

| Parameter | Tolerance |
|-----------|-----------|
| Projection from shell | ±6mm (±1/4 in.) |
| Flange tilt (≤NPS 12) | ±1 degree |
| Flange tilt (>NPS 12) | ±1/2 degree |
| Flange face alignment | ±3mm (±1/8 in.) at OD |
| Bolt hole orientation | ±3mm (±1/8 in.) |

### 5.2 Roof/Bottom Nozzle Tolerances

| Parameter | Tolerance |
|-----------|-----------|
| Projection from shell to flange face | ±6mm (±1/4 in.) |
| Flange tilt (all sizes) | ±1/2 degree |

### 5.3 Flange Bolt Hole Orientation

- Shell nozzles: Bolt holes shall straddle the flange vertical centerline
- Roof/floor nozzles: Bolt holes shall straddle flange centerlines

## 7. Drain Pipe Requirements

### 7.1 Swing Drain Specifications (Annex H)

- Fabricated from Schedule 80 pipe
- Steel swing or pivot-jointed pipe drains
- Designed for thermal movement

### 7.2 Floor Drain Coupling

- Threaded pipe couplings with 600mm (24") minimum spacing from shell
- Provision for draining and venting required

## 8. Roof Column Specifications (Section 5.10)

### 8.1 Pipe Columns

- Made from pipe or structural shapes
- Pipe columns shall be sealed or have openings for drainage
- Preferred for internal floating roof tanks

## 9. Welding Requirements

### 9.1 Fillet Weld Sizes

**Nozzle-to-Shell Welds:**
- Outside weld: Full penetration or fillet = 0.7 × tn (minimum)
- Inside weld (if accessible): Seal weld

**Flange-to-Nozzle Welds:**
- Slip-on: Fillet weld both sides, throat = tn or Tf (lesser)
- Welding-neck: Full penetration groove weld

### 9.2 Stress Relief Requirements

For carbon steel:
- Shell plates > 32mm (1-1/4 in.): PWHT required
- Flange-to-neck welds > 19mm (3/4 in.): PWHT consideration
