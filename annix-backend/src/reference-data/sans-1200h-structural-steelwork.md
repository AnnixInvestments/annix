# SANS 1200 H - Structural Steelwork

> Source: SABS 1200 H-1990 (Civil Engineering Construction)
> This document contains key reference data extracted for use by the Annix structural steel module.

## 1. Overview

SANS 1200 H provides requirements for structural steelwork in civil engineering construction including:
- Material specifications (steel grades, bolts, rivets, welding consumables)
- Fabrication requirements (cutting, holes, assembly)
- Tolerances for fabrication and erection
- Testing and inspection requirements
- Measurement and payment provisions

**Scope:** Structural steelwork for buildings and civil engineering structures (excluding cranes and bridges which have separate standards).

## 2. Material Requirements

### 2.1 Structural Steel Grades (SABS 1431)

| Grade | Description | Max Thickness for Brittle Fracture |
|-------|-------------|-----------------------------------|
| 300WA | Carbon-manganese steel | 25 mm |
| 350WA | Higher strength C-Mn steel | 20 mm |
| 450WA | High strength C-Mn steel | 12 mm |
| 300WC | Impact tested C-Mn steel | No limit (with impact test) |
| 350WC | Impact tested higher strength | No limit (with impact test) |

Note: For thicknesses exceeding limits, impact testing (27 J at 0°C) is required.

### 2.2 Welding Consumables (SABS 455)

| Application | Electrode Type | Requirement |
|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| C-Mn steel | SABS 455 electrodes | Matching strength class |
| Shop welding | All positions | Position qualified |
| Site welding | Specific approval | Weather protection required |

### 2.3 Bolts, Nuts and Washers

| Type | Standard | Application |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Hexagon bolts (coarse thread) | SABS 135 | General structural |
| Precision hexagon bolts | SABS 136 | Fitted connections |
| Mushroom/countersunk bolts | SABS 1143 | Specific applications |
| Flat washers | SABS 1149 | Under bolt heads/nuts |
| Taper washers | SABS 1149 | On tapered flanges |
| Friction-grip bolts | SABS 1282 | High-strength connections |

### 2.4 Bolt Grades and Tension

| Bolt Grade | Connection Type | Tension Requirement |
|------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Grade 4.6 | Shear/bearing | Hand-tight (podger spanner) |
| Grade 8.8 | Shear/bearing | Hand-tight (podger spanner) |
| High tensile | Major tension/moment | 75% of proof load stress |
| Friction-grip | SABS 094 | Turn-of-the-nut method |

### 2.5 Friction-Grip Fastener Reuse

| Grade | Condition | Reuse Allowed |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Grade 8.8s | Black (ungalvanized) | Yes, at Engineer's discretion |
| Grade 8.8s | Galvanized | No |
| Grade 10.9s | Any | No |

### 2.6 Rivets (SABS 435)

Mild steel rivets for structural connections where specified.

### 2.7 Structural Hollow Sections (SABS 657)

Part I: Steel tubes for scaffolding and structural/general engineering purposes.

## 3. Fabrication Requirements

### 3.1 Cutting Methods

| Method | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| Sawing | Permitted |
| Shearing | Permitted |
| Cropping | Permitted |
| Machine flame-cutting | Permitted |
| Manual flame-cutting | Only where authorized |

### 3.2 Edge Requirements

- Edges shall be free from defects or distortions
- All exposed burrs shall be ground off and smoothed
- Flame-cut edges may require dressing to remove notches

### 3.3 Holes for Fasteners

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Formation method | Drilled (preferred) |
| Punching limit | Material ≤ hole diameter in thickness |
| Punching thick material | > 12 mm requires prior approval |
| Flame-cutting holes | Not permitted (except HD bolts with approval) |

### 3.4 Hole Clearances

| Fastener Type | Fastener Diameter | Maximum Clearance |
|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Standard bolts/rivets | ≤ 24 mm | 2 mm |
| Standard bolts/rivets | > 24 mm | 3 mm |
| Friction-grip (outer) | All | 2 mm |
| Friction-grip (inner, >3 parts) | All | 3 mm |
| Fitted bolts/pins | All | 0 to +0.1 mm |

### 3.5 Fitted Bolt Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Hole diameter | Equal to bolt diameter (0 to +0.1 mm tolerance) |
| Thread position | No threaded portion within bearing thickness |
| Washer | Required under nut |
| Drilling | Through assembled parts in single operation |
| Sub-drilling | At least 2 mm undersize, then ream |

### 3.6 Slotted Holes

| Method | Condition |
|--------|-----------|
| Drill/punch pair + flame-cut | General |
| Slot punching | Material ≤ 12 mm thickness |

## 4. Assembly Requirements

### 4.1 Bolt Length Requirements

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| Thread projection | At least 1 thread beyond nut |
| Clear thread | At least 1 full thread between nut and shank |

### 4.2 Washer Requirements

| Condition | Washer Required |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Flat surfaces, normal clearance | Not required (unless specified) |
| Tapered flanges | Taper washers required |
| Greater than normal clearance | Washers under head and nut |
| Fitted bolts | Under nut |

## 6. Riveting Requirements

### 6.1 Rivet Installation

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| Equipment | Pneumatic or hydraulic |
| Heating | Uniform throughout length |
| Condition when driven | Bright red heat, free from scale |
| Hole filling | Complete |

### 6.2 Rivet Defect Limits

| Defect Type | Rejection Criterion |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Eccentricity | > 0.1 × shank diameter |
| Pitting (burning) | > 1 mm depth over > 10% surface |
| Cracks | Any visible to unaided eye |
| Gap under head | > 0.15 mm feeler gauge to shank |
| Looseness | Movement felt when struck |
| Malformed head | "Jockey cap" head |
| Countersunk unfilled | Not fully filled |

### 6.3 Defect Tolerance

- Maximum 5% of rivets in single joint may have lesser magnitude defects
- No two such defective rivets closer than 10 rivet pitches

## 8. Setting-Out and Erection

### 8.1 Pre-Erection Checks

- Verify setting-out of holding-down bolts
- Check levels of concrete foundations
- Report discrepancies to Engineer immediately

### 8.2 Erection Procedure

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| Procedure submission | When required by project specification |
| Storage | Prevent damage, excessive stress, keep clean |
| Temporary bracing | As required for stability |
| Alignment | As soon as possible after erection |

### 8.3 Corrections During Erection

| Action | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| Drift pins/jacking | Not for improperly fabricated members |
| Reaming/cutting | Moderate degree for minor misfits only |
| Burning holes | Not permitted without Engineer's consent |

## 9. Grouting of Supports

### 9.1 Stanchions in Foundation Pockets

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Corrosion protection | Applied to 100 mm below concrete level |
| Additional protection | 100 mm above and below concrete level |
| Grout strength | ≥ surrounding concrete or 20 MPa |
| Maximum aggregate | 10 mm |
| Initial fill | At least 2/3 of pocket depth |
| Waiting period | At least 48 hours before filling remainder |

### 9.2 Encasing Steelwork

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Concrete cover | Minimum 100 mm |
| Concrete type | Dense concrete |

## 10. Testing and Inspection

- Steel test certificates

|-----------|----------|

## 11. Sundry Items

### 11.1 Open Grid Floor Deflection

| Parameter | Limit |
|-----------|-------|
| Deflection under design load | Lesser of: L/200 or 10 mm |

All hollow sections shall be sealed to prevent moisture ingress unless special corrosion protection is provided.
